Ten best life lessons

Read time: 7 minutes

1. Learning to say No [being assertive]

No is your ultimate boundary, it is the gate that stops invaders from entering your heart,soul and mind.

You are not a chamelion nor a people pleaser, yes social settings are important but not to the point of fragmenting your whole identity.

Even look a children, a baby does not say "Please I'm hungry", they just scream, we are all born assertive, it is a skill we all have.

Some of us lose it along the way but there is still hope of recovering it, since it is an inherent human trait

My favourite quote relating to people pleasing is "I would sacrifice pieces of my flesh and still be considered selfish for not giving my bones."

2. Financial Freedom

Money gives you power and the freedom to make choice & to have a voice

Studies have shown money direclty shapes your life expectancy

In order to have financial freedom, you need financial wisdom

Read financial books, I am currently reading "Girls that invest" by Simran Kaur, haha and dont worry you dont have to be a girl to read this!

Girls that Invest offical website

.

3. Student & Teacher for life

School is not forever, but you will always be a student

Education is for life, you must constantly challenge your thinking

Gaining knowledge gived you humility and compassion

Haha you can hate school system but NEVER hate education

.

4. It is okay to rest

We live in a culture that is fast-pace and constantly work, work WORK!

Resting is a form of work, just passive! Take care of yourself and breathe

It is better to be stagnant than to regress

.

5. Goals, legacy

Picture this! You lost your birth certificate, so legal evidence of your existence is now removed. You are in court, debating your existence. What would you say?

Whatever you say is how you see yourself and the legacy that you will leave behind

For example: I am mother! My children know I exist. You value your motherhood as both an identity [present] and legacy [future]

Therefore consider this court scenario, what kind of legacy do you wish to leave

.

6. "Everyone was a slave to something" -- Kenny Ackerman, AOT

Choose your slavery well, what will control you?

Money, power, lust, family, love, God

Humans will always gravitate to something, something to fill us up, choose your "slavery" well

.

7. Two of my favourite books

The Bible & My Diary

I am a Chrisitian, therefore I value and find life in the Bible

Journalling is key, it helps you self-reflect, regulate and analyse

Plus it is kind of like a time capusle, you go back to your memories

Thank you for reading and hope you learnt something new OR were at least able to regurgitate it.

Praise S.Imaikop
